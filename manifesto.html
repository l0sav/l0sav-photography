The Art of Noticing

A manifesto for paying attention.

I believe the world becomes larger when I pay closer attention to it.

  1. I choose curiosity before perfection.

    The most meaningful moments rarely announce themselves. I would rather remain open than wait for ideal conditions.

  2. I let the experience come first.

    The photograph matters, but it is not the reason to hurry through a place. Presence is not a delay; it is the point.

  3. I look beyond the famous view.

    California is more than its landmarks. It is fog, backroads, working towns, oak hills, shorelines, insects, rivers, forests, and lives unfolding between destinations.

  4. I treat small lives as worthy of attention.

    A caterpillar, a bee, a weathered tree, or a flower in an overlooked corner can carry an entire story.

  5. I try to leave places—and people—better than I found them.

    Sometimes that means picking up litter. Sometimes it means holding a door. Care does not need to be dramatic to matter.

  6. I share beauty as an invitation.

    Not to say, “Look what I saw,” but to ask, “What might you notice next?”

Create the kind of work that reminds people the world is still worth paying attention to.

I’ll see you out there.
— Lauren
